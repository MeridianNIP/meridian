Authentication — AD group → Meridian role
After the bind account reads AD, the mappings below decide which Meridian role an AD-authenticated user gets. On login we read the user's memberOf; if any of their groups matches a row here, they inherit the highest-privilege role among all matches. Without a mapping and without Auto-create, AD users can't log in even if the bind account can read them.
AD group DN → Role
Anyone in one of the mapped AD groups can log in with their AD credentials and gets the matching Meridian role — no pre-provisioning step. On first login Meridian silently creates an internal shadow record (no local password, primary_auth=ldap) to anchor the user's sessions, audit trail, preferences, and last-login timestamp. The role is re-read from AD on every login, so adding/removing a user from an AD group takes effect immediately without touching Meridian.
